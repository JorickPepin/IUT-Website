Living in Dijon

Dijon, the radiant capital. Capital of a powerful duchy with a European dimension at the end of the Middle Ages, Dijon today possesses a unique architectural and cultural heritage, magnificently preserved. The protected area of Dijon, a city of art and history, is, on nearly 100 hectares, one of the largest in France. Since July 2015, it has been listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site within the framework of the Climats du vignoble de Bourgogne (Burgundy Vineyard Climates).

Gastronomy

Another treasure to be discovered when you live in Burgundy: gastronomy! Are you thinking mustard? Not false, but don't forget all the rest! Dijon blackcurrant, snails, gingerbread, oeufs meurette, beef bourguignon, fondue bourguignonne, pears poached in wine, parsleyed ham, coq au vin, filet mignon à l'époisses and many other dishes delight the taste buds of all gourmets who live in or come to the region.

And what about the wines? If one or the other can be found in many local dishes, many accompany them perfectly: Pommard, Vosne-Romanée, Meursault, Clos-Vougeot, Chablis Vaudesir, Chambertin, Musigny, Morgon... You just have to choose!
